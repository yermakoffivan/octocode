The missing piece — and the one that surprised us most. You can have the best model and the cleanest tool surface, and your agent will still feel shallow if it doesn't have the organization's understanding. Code can't answer the why-questions on its own — ownership, trade-offs, expired reasoning, "we don't do that here anymore" — that lives outside the repo. We started calling that body of knowledge "context" or "the brain". Three things to know. (1) What it is: skills, prompts, MCP hints, runbooks — reusable playbooks that capture how investigations actually work here, encoded once and run by both humans and agents. (2) Where it comes from: engineers write it. Hand-curated, PR-reviewed, versioned with the rest of the system. Not auto-generated, not scraped — and that's the point: opinionated context is the value. (3) Who uses it: engineers reach for the same skills during manual triage, and agents inherit them on every run. So our tribal knowledge is no longer locked in heads. The net: models scale, tools scale, but context doesn't scale until someone writes it down. The brain is a deliverable.
